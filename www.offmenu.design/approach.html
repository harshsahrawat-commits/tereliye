Creativity is the last bastion of human exceptionalism.

AI models regress to the mean. Code may flow freely. Knowing what to build does not.

That changes the nature of the work.

Ideas can be tested, expanded, and pushed in multiple directions almost immediately. Possibilities that would normally stay unexplored become part of the process. Patterns appear earlier. Weak directions reveal themselves quickly.

The challenge is no longer producing options.

It's knowing which direction deserves to be taken further. Our experience creates clarity. Recognizing when something carries the idea clearly enough to build on, and when it doesn't.

The result is a process that moves quickly without becoming careless. Broader exploration, careful refinement, and work that arrives at the right place with far less guesswork.

Because when you can build anything... How it feels, well, that's everything.

1

Exploration is cheap. Judgment is not.

AI allows us to explore directions quickly and in parallel. Our process takes advantage of that while our experience guides us to the ideas worth evolving.

2

Speed requires structure.

Working faster increases the need for a clear process. We organize exploration deliberately so that iteration produces understanding, not noise.

3

Technology expands the search space.

AI allows us to explore a broader surface. We're no longer constrained to just the browser. We can iterate across the entire stack and beyond.

4

Strong ideas survive pressure.

When many directions are explored, weak ideas reveal themselves quickly. The strongest concepts continue to hold up as they are tested, expanded, and refined.

5

Craft makes the work convincing.

Even in an accelerated process, the final work must feel precise and considered. Craft is what turns a promising direction into something clear and compelling.
